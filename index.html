Our aerodynamic package was developed through iterative CFD simulation in SimScale, analysing pressure coefficient distributions across the body, nose cone, and wheel pods. Each iteration reduced drag while maintaining structural mass targets.

The teardrop nose profile was selected after testing six alternatives — it minimises the frontal pressure stagnation point and guides airflow cleanly to the underbody channel.

CFD Analysis Pressure Mapping Boundary Layer Vortex Control Drag Reduction SimScale
Nose Cone
Elliptical taper with a 3:1 aspect ratio minimises stagnation pressure at the leading edge and encourages attached laminar flow.
Underbody Channel
Venturi-shaped floor generates a low-pressure zone beneath the car, producing meaningful downforce at race velocity without a drag penalty.
Rear Taper & Wing
Progressive boat-tail taper delays flow separation. The minimal rear wing adds directional stability without measurable Cd increase.

// Manufacturing

PRECISION BUILT

🪵
Material Selection
Competition-grade balsa wood selected for its exceptional strength-to-weight ratio. Each blank density-tested before cutting to guarantee consistency across runs.
Density: 0.12 g/cm³
⚙️
CNC Machining
3-axis CNC router with 0.05 mm positional accuracy translates the CAD model directly to the physical blank. Zero hand-shaping errors introduced.
Tolerance: ±0.05 mm
✨
Surface Finishing
Progressive wet-sanding from 220 to 2000 grit followed by polishing compound brings surface roughness below Ra 0.4 μm — measurably reducing skin friction drag.
Ra < 0.4 μm
⚖️
Mass Management
Every gram is tracked. Axle holes precision-drilled for minimum play. The final car comes in at 54 g — within the target window for optimised CO₂ acceleration.
Final Mass: 54 g
🎨
Livery & Branding
Water-slide decals applied over a sealed surface preserve aerodynamics while delivering a professional team identity that judges recognise from first glance.
Paint layer: <0.5 g
🔩
Axle & Wheel System
Polished stainless axles seated in drilled channels. Wheels balanced by hand and checked for runout. Rotational drag reduced by 8% vs prior design generation.
Runout: < 0.1 mm

// Race Strategy

LAUNCH PROTOCOL

⏱️
Reaction Time Training
Our driver trains with digital start-light simulators to sharpen response below 200 ms. Consistent sub-150 ms times recorded in practice sessions.
📐
CO₂ Canister Seating
Canister inserted to a consistent depth using a depth-stop tool, guaranteeing identical puncture alignment across every race run for repeatable launch force.
🎯
Track Alignment
Car placed on track 30 seconds before run to allow axle temperature equilibration. Alignment checked with a sighting tool against the guide string.
🔁
Run Consistency
Standard pre-run checklist executed before every attempt. Variance across 10 practice runs: <3 ms — a statistical benchmark for mechanical repeatability.
